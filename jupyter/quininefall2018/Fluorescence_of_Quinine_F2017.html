Sample Preparation and Data Collection¶

A 1 ppm quinine stock solution was prepared by Jimena. We diluted this 1/20 to a concentration of 50 ppb with 0.050 M H2SO4. An unknown sample and control sample were prepared for standard addition in the following samples.

  1. Blank (0.050 M H2SO4)
  2. 5 mL diluted Control
  3. 5 mL diluted Control + 4 mL 50.00 g/L quinine
  4. 5 mL diluted U19C
  5. 5 mL diluted U19C + 1 mL 50.00 g/L quinine
  6. 5 mL diluted U19C + 2 mL 50.00 g/L quinine
  7. 5 mL diluted U19C + 3 mL 50.00 g/L quinine
  8. 5 mL diluted U19C + 4 mL 50.00 g/L quinine
  9. 5 mL diluted U53A
  10. 5 mL diluted U53A + 1 mL 50.00 g/L quinine
  11. 5 mL diluted U53A + 2 mL 50.00 g/L quinine
  12. 5 mL diluted U53A + 3 mL 50.00 g/L quinine
  13. 5 mL diluted U53A + 4 mL 50.00 g/L quinine
  14. 5 mL diluted U57B
  15. 5 mL diluted U57B + 1 mL 50.00 g/L quinine
  16. 5 mL diluted U57B + 2 mL 50.00 g/L quinine
  17. 5 mL diluted U57B + 3 mL 50.00 g/L quinine
  18. 5 mL diluted U57B + 4 mL 50.00 g/L quinine

Each sample was QS'd to 10 mL in a scintillation vial prior to fourescence measurement.

Standard Addition with one addition: (note how the slope cancels) \begin{equation} S_x = mC_x \end{equation}

\begin{equation} S_{x+s} = m(C_x+C_s) \end{equation}

\begin{equation} \frac{S_x}{S_{x+s}} = \frac{mC_x}{m(C_x+C_s)} \end{equation}

\begin{equation} \frac{113.49}{312.06} = \frac{C_x}{C_x+20} \end{equation}

\begin{equation} S_{ratio} = \frac{C_x}{C_x+20} \end{equation}

\begin{equation} C_x = \frac{S_{ratio}*20}{1-S_{ratio}} \end{equation}
